Mobility and Justice in Brasília

This project explores mobility in Brasília as an ethical and political issue, analysing how accessibility inequalities are connected to territorial segregation and institutional decisions.

Why Brasília?

Brasília is a planned city that embodies profound spatial inequalities. Long distances, car-oriented design, and peripheralisation of low-income populations make mobility a key factor shaping access to opportunities.

Theoretical framework

The analysis combines the concept of social exclusion with Rawlsian distributive justice and the Capability Approach to assess when mobility inequalities become morally problematic.

Main insights

  • Mobility inequalities translate into inequality of opportunities
  • Territorial segregation reinforces social exclusion
  • Justice in transport requires prioritising disadvantaged groups
