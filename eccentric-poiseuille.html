Analytical Hagen–Poiseuille Flow in Eccentric Semiannular Ducts

A research-oriented project developing an exact analytical solution for steady, incompressible laminar flow in a non-standard duct geometry using bipolar coordinates.

Project overview

Hagen–Poiseuille flow is a classical benchmark in fluid mechanics: a steady, fully developed, incompressible laminar flow driven by a constant pressure gradient. While exact solutions are well known for circular pipes and annular ducts, many practical cross-sections are not covered by standard textbooks.

This project develops an exact analytical solution for Poiseuille flow in eccentric semiannular ducts, a geometry that combines a semiannulus with an offset inner boundary. The work addresses a gap by providing closed-form expressions that can be used as a reference for numerical validation and engineering analysis.

Problem statement
Derive the velocity field and flow-rate-related quantities for fully developed laminar flow in an eccentric semiannular duct, and quantify how eccentricity influences the hydraulic response of the system.

Method and assumptions

The model considers steady, incompressible, Newtonian flow under a constant axial pressure gradient. With fully developed conditions, the governing equation reduces to a Poisson problem for the axial velocity on the cross-section with no-slip boundary conditions.

To handle the eccentric semiannular boundaries analytically, the cross-section is represented in bipolar coordinates, enabling boundary-fitted formulation and separation of variables. The solution is constructed to satisfy the boundary conditions and the forcing term associated with the pressure gradient.

  • Steady, incompressible, Newtonian fluid
  • Fully developed laminar flow (velocity varies only across the cross-section)
  • No-slip at solid boundaries
  • Constant pressure gradient driving the flow

Key outputs

The analytical formulation provides expressions that enable evaluation of the duct’s hydraulic behaviour across different eccentricity configurations. Main outputs include:

  • Closed-form velocity distribution over the cross-section
  • Volumetric flow rate as a function of geometry parameters
  • Dimensionless performance indicators (e.g., Poiseuille-type numbers)
  • Qualitative and quantitative influence of eccentricity on resistance to flow

Why it matters

Exact analytical solutions are valuable because they function as benchmarks: they help validate numerical solvers, support sensitivity analysis, and provide insight into how geometry drives hydraulic losses. Eccentric semiannular geometries can appear in constrained passages, partially obstructed ducts, and specialized channel designs.

By extending classical duct-flow solutions to a previously unexplored cross-section, this project strengthens the analytical toolkit available for both research and engineering applications.
